About This App

Hotstepper is a fitness and step tracking application that helps you monitor your daily physical activity. This privacy policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your personal information.

Health Data Collection

This application requests access to your health and fitness data through Google Health Connect (Android) or Apple HealthKit (iOS) to provide you with activity tracking features. We collect the following types of health data:

  • Steps: Daily step count to track your walking activity
  • Distance: Distance traveled during physical activities
  • Calories: Active calories burned during activities
  • Location Data: GPS coordinates during active sessions (only when you start a session)

Personal Information Collected

In addition to health data, we collect:

  • Account Information: Email address, display name
  • Profile Data: Height, weight, age, gender (optional, for calorie calculations)
  • Device Information: Device type, push notification tokens
  • Usage Data: App interactions, session history, streak data

How We Use Your Data

Your data is used exclusively to:

  • Display your daily activity progress and statistics
  • Track your fitness goals and maintain streak records
  • Show your position on the leaderboard (only if you opt-in)
  • Provide personalized activity insights and recommendations
  • Send you push notifications (reminders, achievements) if enabled
  • Improve app functionality and user experience

Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services to operate this application:

  • Supabase (Backend Infrastructure): Provides database storage, user authentication, and serverless functions. Your data is stored on Supabase servers. Supabase Privacy Policy
  • Google Health Connect: Used to read health and fitness data from your Android device. Health Connect Privacy
  • Google Maps API: Used to display maps during active walking sessions. Google Privacy Policy
  • Firebase Cloud Messaging: Used to send push notifications to your device. Firebase Privacy Policy
  • Apple HealthKit (iOS): Used to read health and fitness data on iOS devices. Apple Privacy Policy

Data Storage & Security

Your data is protected using the following measures:

  • All data is encrypted in transit using TLS/HTTPS
  • Data at rest is encrypted on secure cloud servers
  • User authentication is handled securely with industry-standard practices
  • We implement Row Level Security (RLS) to ensure you can only access your own data
  • Passwords are hashed and never stored in plain text

Data Retention

We retain your data as follows:

  • Account Data: Retained for as long as your account is active
  • Health & Activity Data: Retained for up to 2 years for historical tracking
  • Session Data: Retained for 1 year
  • Upon Account Deletion: All personal data is permanently deleted within 30 days

Data Sharing

We do NOT sell, rent, or share your personal or health data with third parties for marketing or advertising purposes.

Your data may only be shared:

  • On the leaderboard (only if you explicitly enable this feature in settings)
  • In aggregated, anonymized form for app improvement and analytics
  • With the third-party services listed above, solely for app functionality
  • When required by law or valid legal process

Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access: View all data we have collected about you (via the Export feature)
  • Portability: Download your data in a machine-readable format (JSON)
  • Deletion: Permanently delete your account and all associated data
  • Revoke Permissions: Revoke health data access at any time
  • Opt-Out: Disable leaderboard visibility and push notifications
  • Correction: Update or correct your profile information

Revoking Health Data Permissions

To revoke this app's access to your health data:

On Android:

  1. Open the Health Connect app on your device
  2. Go to "App permissions"
  3. Find Hotstepper and tap it
  4. Revoke the permissions you no longer wish to grant

On iOS:

  1. Open the Settings app
  2. Tap "Health" → "Data Access & Devices"
  3. Find Hotstepper and manage permissions
